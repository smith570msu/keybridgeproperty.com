St. Clair County's housing market

St. Clair County is the northeastern edge of Metro Detroit's footprint, anchored by Port Huron on the St. Clair River at the Canadian border. The county has a mix of industrial-adjacent communities, waterfront properties along the river and Lake St. Clair, and rural townships. It's a smaller market than Wayne or Macomb but an active one for cash buyers given the county's older housing stock.

Port Huron and the river communities

Port Huron, Marysville, and Marine City are the most active markets in the county. Port Huron has a significant stock of older homes, some needing significant work, where a cash sale often makes more sense than a renovation-and-list strategy. The river communities -- Marine City, Algonac -- have waterfront properties that can be difficult to finance and are natural fits for cash buyers.

Rural St. Clair County

The inland townships are largely agricultural with scattered rural residential. We buy throughout the county, including properties that are difficult to reach through traditional channels due to condition or location.
