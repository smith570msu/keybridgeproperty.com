Speed without sacrifice

A traditional listing in Metro Detroit takes 30 to 90 days to close after an accepted offer -- and that assumes the buyer's financing holds. By the time you factor in prep work, showings, inspections, and the negotiation after the inspection, most sellers are looking at four to six months from decision to done.

With Keybridge, the timeline looks like this:

  • Day 1: You submit the form or call. We gather the address and a few details about condition.
  • Within 24 hours: We research comparable sales and make a written, no-obligation cash offer.
  • Days 2-5: If you accept, we open escrow and order title. You don't need to do anything.
  • Closing: As fast as about a week from acceptance, or later if you need more time. Your date.

Who needs to sell fast

Speed matters most when circumstances don't allow a long runway. The situations we see most often: a job relocation with a start date, a foreclosure notice with a court date, an estate that needs to be settled before heirs can move on, a divorce where both parties want out of a shared asset, or a landlord who is done managing a problem property.

In every case, the math is the same. Holding costs -- mortgage, taxes, insurance, utilities -- run $1,000 to $2,500 per month on a typical Metro Detroit home. Every month you wait costs money. A lower cash offer that closes in a week often nets more than a higher listed price that closes in four months.

What "as-is" means in practice

We buy the house exactly as it sits. No cleaning, no repairs, no staging. Take the belongings you want and leave the rest -- we handle disposal. There are no repair credits negotiated at closing, no inspection contingencies, and no lender appraisal to satisfy.

Service area

We buy houses throughout southeastern Michigan, including Wayne, Oakland, Macomb, Washtenaw, Livingston, Monroe, and St. Clair counties.

Questions

Straight answers.

How quickly can we really close?
The legal minimum in Michigan is roughly 5 to 7 business days once title is clear. If you need more time -- weeks or months -- that's fine too. You set the date.
Do you buy houses in any condition?
Yes. Fire damage, foundation issues, code violations, hoarding situations, tenant-occupied -- we've bought all of these. Condition does affect the offer, but nothing disqualifies a property.
Will I net more selling fast versus listing?
Sometimes yes, sometimes no. It depends on the property's condition, how much you'd spend on repairs to list, and your holding costs. We're happy to walk through the math with you.
Is there any obligation once I get an offer?
None. The offer is free, in writing, and expires in 7 days. If you decline, that's the end of it.
What counties do you serve?
Wayne, Oakland, Macomb, Washtenaw, Livingston, Monroe, and St. Clair counties.
