Livingston County's housing market

Livingston County is one of Michigan's fastest-growing counties, with strong residential demand driven by families relocating from Wayne and Oakland counties. The county's newer housing stock means fewer distressed properties than in the inner-ring suburbs, but situations still arise: estates, divorces, job relocations, and landlords in Howell and Brighton who are ready to exit.

Rural and semi-rural properties

A significant portion of Livingston County is rural or semi-rural, with properties on acreage, well and septic systems, and older farmhouses. We buy these. Rural properties can be harder to move through traditional channels -- fewer comparable sales, harder to finance for retail buyers -- making a cash sale a practical choice.

Howell and Brighton

Howell and Brighton are the county's two main population centers, with active retail markets. For properties in good condition in these communities, a traditional listing is worth considering. Where we add the most value is properties that need work, estates, and sellers who need to close fast.
